# YouTube Script Writing

## What This Format Is

A YouTube script is not a spoken article. It's a retention machine — structured specifically to keep the viewer watching long enough for the algorithm to distribute the video widely. Average view duration (AVD) and click-through rate (CTR) are the two metrics YouTube optimizes for. The script exists to maximize both.

This format works for: educational content, explainer videos, listicles, case studies, and opinion pieces. It does NOT work for live-stream content, purely conversational videos, or content that deliberately violates hook-retention conventions (occasionally fine for established creators, not for growth).

---

## Structure

### 0-30 seconds: The Hook (determines click-through AND retention)

The hook does two jobs simultaneously: it rewards the viewer for clicking (CTR optimization) and it creates a reason to keep watching (AVD optimization).

**Hook structure:**
1. **The Pattern Interrupt** (0-3 seconds): A visual or verbal surprise that stops passive scrolling. A bold claim, an unexpected image, a question, or a counterintuitive statement.
2. **The Problem Statement** (3-10 seconds): Articulate the viewer's problem more precisely than they could. "If you've ever lost a deal you thought you had, and you still don't know why, this video is for you."
3. **The Promise** (10-20 seconds): What will they be able to do by the end? Specific. Measurable. Not "you'll learn about X" but "by the end you'll have a framework for X."
4. **The Credibility Bridge** (20-30 seconds, optional): A brief signal of why you're qualified to teach this — a specific result, a credential, or a noteworthy observation. Skip if it feels forced.

**What kills hook retention:** Lengthy intros ("Today we're going to be talking about..."), begging for likes before delivering value, channel plugs before the content starts.

---

### First Third: Setup (establish the problem + stakes)

**Job:** Make the viewer care about what's coming. Establish what they stand to gain or lose.

**Techniques:**
- State the common mistake/misconception clearly
- Use a story or case example that makes the stakes tangible
- Preview the key insight without fully revealing it (open loop)

**Pacing markers:** Keep paragraphs short — 2-3 sentences maximum. Script should feel like it can be delivered in 150-160 words per minute for natural-sounding speech.

---

### Middle Third: Value Delivery

**Job:** Deliver the core content with enough specificity and structure to be genuinely useful.

**Structure options:**
- Step-by-step framework (numbered, each step clearly distinct)
- Compare/contrast (what most people do vs. what actually works)
- Case study (problem → what was tried → what worked → why)

**Retention technique — mid-video hook:** At the 40-50% mark, add a brief re-engagement moment. "And the next part is where most people get stuck — stay with me." This combats natural drop-off at the midpoint.

**B-roll notes:** Script should indicate where B-roll or screenshare is needed — not as decoration but as a replacement for talking-head explanation. "SHOW SCREEN: [what to show]" in the script.

---

### Final Third: Payoff + CTA

**Job:** Land the most memorable insight and direct the viewer's next action.

**Payoff:** The final teaching point should be the single most surprising or actionable thing in the video. Don't save energy — viewers remember the ending.

**CTA structure:**
1. Verbal CTA (subscribe / like / comment) — brief, 10-15 seconds
2. End screen CTA (next video recommendation) — "If you found this useful, watch this next:" pointing to a highly related video
3. Card CTAs — placed 20-30 seconds before the end, pointing to related content mid-video

**What to avoid:** Multiple competing CTAs. Long outro music. Extended "thanks for watching."

---

## Rules

- Write the hook LAST — after you know the full content, write the hook that best sets it up.
- Script is for pacing, not teleprompter reading. Conversational language, active voice.
- Every section transition has a sentence that bridges to the next: "Now that you understand X, here's why that matters for Y."
- Open loops must close. Every question or promise raised in the hook must be answered before the CTA.
- Script length target: ~150 words per minute of planned video length.

## Pacing Reference

| Video Length | Word Count Target | Structure |
|-------------|-------------------|-----------|
| 5-7 min | 750-1050 words | Hook + 2 sections + CTA |
| 8-12 min | 1200-1800 words | Hook + 3-4 sections + CTA |
| 15-20 min | 2250-3000 words | Hook + 5-6 sections + CTA |
| 20+ min | 3000+ words | Hook + 6+ sections, mid-video hook required |
