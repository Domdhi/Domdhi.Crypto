# LinkedIn Post Writing

## What This Format Is

A LinkedIn post lives or dies at the hook line — the first 1-2 sentences visible before "...see more." If those sentences don't create a reason to click, the rest of the post doesn't exist. The platform's algorithm rewards posts that get clicks, dwell time, and comments — in that order. This format is engineered around those three signals.

LinkedIn is distinct from other platforms in one important way: professional credibility is the currency. Posts that work on LinkedIn combine professional insight with personal honesty — the "I learned this the hard way" structure performs better than the "here are the best practices" structure because the former signals real experience.

---

## Structure

### Hook Line (the only line that guarantees a read)

**Visible before truncation:** Approximately 140-150 characters on desktop, 90-100 on mobile. Write for mobile — 90 characters max for the most important idea.

**Hook patterns that work:**
- Counterintuitive statement: "The most expensive mistake in hiring isn't a bad hire. It's a slow hire."
- Specific observation: "I've reviewed 400 proposals. 80% lose on the first page."
- Provocative number: "We grew from $0 to $40k MRR without a single paid ad."
- Uncomfortable truth: "Most founders aren't failing because of competition. They're failing because of prioritization."

**What kills the hook:** Starting with "I'm excited to share..." or "I wanted to talk about..." or any opener that delays the point. Also: questions that don't create genuine urgency.

**The cliffhanger rule:** The hook should create a question that the reader needs to scroll to answer. If the hook is complete in itself, there's no reason to click "see more."

---

### Body: 3-5 Paragraphs

**Paragraph length:** 1-3 sentences per paragraph. Single-sentence paragraphs are common and effective — they create white space that makes posts scannable on mobile.

**Structure options:**

**Narrative (story-driven):**
Para 1: The setup (situation/context)
Para 2: The problem or turning point
Para 3: What I tried / what happened
Para 4: The insight that came from it
Para 5: The takeaway or implication

**Framework (insight-driven):**
Para 1: The main claim
Para 2: First supporting point
Para 3: Second supporting point
Para 4: Third supporting point
Para 5: Implication or call to think

**List hybrid:**
Para 1: The frame ("Here are the 3 things I tell every founder who asks about pricing:")
Paras 2-4: One item per paragraph, brief explanation
Para 5: The closer

---

### Close: Engagement Invitation (not a CTA)

LinkedIn posts perform better when they end with an invitation to conversation rather than a link click. The algorithm rewards comments heavily.

**Effective closes:**
- "What's your experience with this?" (genuine question, not rhetorical)
- "I'm curious if this is just me or if others have seen this too."
- "Drop your take in the comments — I read every reply."

**What to avoid:** Closing with a link to your product/service as the primary CTA. LinkedIn suppresses link posts. If you must include a link, put it in the first comment, not the post body.

---

## Rules

- Hook line: under 90 characters, creates a scroll-to-answer question
- Single-sentence paragraphs are good, not lazy
- No "Let me know your thoughts!" — too generic; ask a specific question
- Links in comments, not post body (if you need to link)
- No hashtag spam — 2-3 relevant hashtags maximum, at the end
- No corporate jargon: "leverage," "synergy," "disruptive," "thought leader"
- Emojis: 0-3 maximum, only where they clarify or replace a phrase, never as decoration

## Algorithm Notes (2025)

- Native documents (carousels) get 2-3x the organic reach of text-only posts
- Video performs well but requires captions (90% of LinkedIn video is watched without sound)
- Polls get reach but low-quality engagement — avoid unless you have a genuine data question
- Posts with early comments (within first 30 minutes) get significant algorithmic boost — respond to early commenters quickly
- Reposting someone else's content with added commentary outperforms cold sharing
